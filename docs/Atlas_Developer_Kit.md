# Atlas Developer Kit

## Purpose
The Atlas is the navigation layer for the architecture. It explains the conceptual structure, relationships, and usage patterns of V5–V7. It is descriptive, not authoritative.

## What the Atlas Is
- A conceptual map of the architecture.
- A glossary of core terms.
- A guide for implementers and integrators.
- A reference for understanding boundaries and flows.

## What the Atlas Is Not
- Not a kernel.
- Not a runtime.
- Not a governance engine.
- Not a source of authority.

## Components
- **Concept Maps:** High-level diagrams of layers and flows.
- **Glossary:** Definitions of mechanics, stability, governance, and related terms.
- **Usage Patterns:** Recommended ways to build on top of the architecture.
- **Anti-Patterns:** Common mistakes that violate stability or governance.

## Developer Usage
- Begin with the Atlas to understand the architecture.
- Use it to identify integration points.
- Use it to avoid misusing layers or bypassing constraints.

## Extensibility
Atlas content may expand with:
- new diagrams,
- new examples,
- new integration patterns.

Extensions must not redefine V5, V6, or V7.
